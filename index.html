4

Business
August 2010 - Present

In search of work that would afford me the time to look after my mum while covering all of our expenses; I started my own business; a dance school. What started as a small class with 30 children quickly grew to a network of over a 5,000 children spanning four boroughs. Mistakes were never far away, but the passion that flourished within me, proved to be the catalyst for organic growth. I employed a team of industry dancers and together we partnered with schools across London. We sold out fringe theatres, liaised with London councils, and harassed heads of education for change. We stuck by it for 5 years before a big problem became too large to ignore.

6

Startup
Febuary 2015 - March 2016

I set out to solve this problem with a bootstrapped education startup. Those three words don’t sit well together – tough times ahead. Together with my technical cofounder, we created a native iOS application that teachers could use to deliver a dance class, quickly, easily and with little fuss – regardless of experience. Think cookbook for dance. I was focused on product and growth; testing, iterating, pitching and plugging. We nailed the problem, but education is notoriously difficult particularly when trying to change things that have remained unchanged for...well forever. We were met with apprehension and lots of closed doors. But we persisted, a few schools at a time, and eventually landed a sizeable deal with a channel partner for 100+ schools. Two weeks until launch and I woke up to a resignation email from my cofounder. He didn’t have a passion for dance or education. I did. He had the technical understanding, and I didn’t. But I had learnt to persist. My passion outweighed any excuse. I didn’t have any of the cool tech, but I was solving a real problem. I managed to complete a deal with our channel partner to sell the content for their team to use. Although it was far from ideal, it was a reward for the time I invested - but not the end.
